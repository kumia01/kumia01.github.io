Keycard

Problem

The challenge we were presented by the Save the Children is as follows.
We are facing an education emergency. Millions of children might not return to school, drop out or not be enrolled at all.
Save the Children needs input and innovative thoughts on how we can improve the work with education management systems to
ensure children's right for education is fullfilled.

How can attendance and enrolement data prevent dropout in school? How can technology play a role in solving this issue?

Solution


Our inspiration lies within basic human rights. Free primary education is a human right, and secondary education should be available to all.
We wish to provide a certain level of security in vulnerable regions. Intuitive way of collecting data to make the process easier.
Focusing on incentives and prevention thru the collected data instead of punishment. Relatively cheap technology that ensures easy installment and use.
Easy access ensures intuitive use – forms a habit of scanning the card. Reward system based on attendance data to keep kids coming to school.

Competition points based on collected data to give kids hope for future education and work. Provide a certain level of security inside the school thru the access card. Benefits for the parents based on collected data, an incentive for the parents to keep their kids in school.
